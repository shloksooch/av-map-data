# The Dispatch — Issue 1 — May 15, 2026

## This Week in Autonomy

Trucks did the heavy lifting this week while the robotaxi headlines
mostly came from Waymo's map, not its press team. Aurora and Kodiak
both turned Texas freight corridors into real driverless revenue, which
is the less glamorous but more durable story. If you only read one
thing, make it the Aurora–McLane launch — a Berkshire subsidiary
putting its supply chain on autonomous trucks is a different kind of
signal than another city announcement.

## 🚀 5 Biggest Deployments This Week

**Aurora launches driverless McLane freight, Dallas ⇄ Houston.** Fully
driverless commercial hauls for a Berkshire Hathaway subsidiary after
280K autonomous miles and 1,400 pilot deliveries, with a human observer
still in-cab per the Paccar agreement. Commercial freight is where
unit economics get real. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/06/berkshire-hathaway-mclane-autonomous-freight-trucking-aurora.html))

**Kodiak AI begins Roehl Transport hauls, Dallas ⇄ Houston.** Four
round trips a week started in late April; Kodiak reported 23,500 hours
of paid driverless operations in Q1 and is targeting long-haul
driverless by year-end. ([GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/05/07/3290645/0/en/Kodiak-Begins-Hauling-Freight-Autonomously-With-Roehl-Transport.html))

**Waymo expands to 1,400+ sq mi across 11 cities.** Coverage growth in
Miami, Austin, Atlanta, Houston, and the SF Bay Area, framed around
FIFA World Cup readiness. Expanding existing cities instead of rushing
new ones is the discipline story here. ([Waymo](https://waymo.com/blog/shorts/growing-to-1400-sqm/))

**Aurora + Volvo open a Dallas–Oklahoma City freight route.** A
200-mile lane, five days a week under supervised autonomy, in the final
validation phase before driverless. ([Aurora IR](https://ir.aurora.tech/news-events/press-releases/detail/137/volvo-and-aurora-launch-autonomous-truck-route-to-oklahoma-city))

**Nuro lands a CPUC Drivered Pilot permit in San Francisco.** Now one
of only five companies holding both a DMV Driverless Testing and a CPUC
Drivered Pilot permit, ahead of the Uber/Lucid service. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/05/nuro-receives-driverless-testing-permit-ahead-of-uber-robotaxi-service-launch/))

## 📊 By the Numbers

- 9 new deployment events tracked this week
- 2 operators (Aurora, Kodiak AI) crossed into fully commercial driverless freight
- 5 Waymo metros flagged for near-term service-area expansion

## 🗺️ City to Watch

**Houston.** It shows up twice this week — a Waymo service-area
expansion toward NRG Stadium and the EaDo / Texas Medical Center
districts, and as the southern anchor of both the Aurora–McLane and
Kodiak–Roehl freight lanes. A single metro carrying both a robotaxi
expansion and two driverless-freight corridors is the clearest
near-term test of mixed autonomous operations at city scale.
